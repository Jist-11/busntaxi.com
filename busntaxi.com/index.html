Haridwar to Delhi

Interesting facts to keep in mind when travelling from Haridwar to Delhi

Food options: There are several restaurants and eateries along the way serving both Indian and international cuisines. Don't forget to try the famous local street food during your trip, such as Aloo Tikki, Chaat, and Lassi.

Accommodation options: There are plenty of accommodation options in Haridwar, Roorkee, Muzaffarnagar, Meerut, and Delhi, ranging from budget hotels to luxury resorts. It is recommended to book your stay in advance, especially during peak tourist season.

Attractions: While traveling from Haridwar to Delhi, you can also plan a visit to several attractions along the way, such as the Roorkee Cantonment, Hastinapur Wildlife Sanctuary, and the Qutub Minar in Delhi.

Traffic: The traffic can be heavy at times, especially during peak tourist season, so plan your trip accordingly and avoid driving during rush hour.

Toll booths: There are several toll booths on this route, so keep some cash handy to pay the toll fees.

Petrol pumps and rest stops: There are several petrol pumps and rest stops along the way where you can take a break, refuel your vehicle, and grab a quick bite.

Best time to visit: The best time to plan a road trip from Haridwar to Delhi is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for travel. During the summer months, the heat can be intense, so plan accordingly.

Road conditions: The road conditions are generally good on this route, with well-maintained highways and some stretches of the road passing through hilly terrain. However, it is recommended to drive carefully and watch out for potholes, especially during the monsoon season.

Route: The most popular route for this road trip is Haridwar - Roorkee - Muzaffarnagar - Meerut - Delhi. From Haridwar, take the NH-334 to reach Roorkee. From Roorkee, take the NH-334B to reach Muzaffarnagar. From Muzaffarnagar, switch to the NH-58 to reach Meerut, and then take the Delhi-Meerut Expressway and reach Delhi.

Distance: The distance between Haridwar and Delhi is approximately 230 km and can be covered in around 5-6 hours depending on the traffic and road conditions.

Overall, a road trip from Haridwar to Delhi can be a pleasant and enjoyable experience, with plenty of natural beauty, cultural diversity, and historical significance along the way.

Interesting facts to keep in mind when travelling from Roorkee to Delhi

Food options: Discover a variety of dining options en route from Roorkee to Delhi. Many local dhabas and restaurants offer delicious North Indian cuisine. Don't miss regional specialties!

Accommodation options: Accommodations are available in towns like Muzaffarnagar and Meerut if you need a break. Delhi offers a vast range of hotels for all budgets.

Attractions: Consider stopping at historical sites in Meerut or exploring local markets. Delhi itself is rich with attractions like India Gate and Red Fort.

Traffic: This route can experience significant traffic, especially nearing Delhi and during peak hours. Plan your travel time accordingly.

Toll booths: Expect multiple toll plazas on the way to Delhi. Keep cash or your FASTag ready.

Petrol pumps and rest stops: Numerous fuel stations and rest areas are available along the highway for breaks and refueling.

Best time to visit: October to March offers the most pleasant weather for this road trip. Summers can be very hot.

Road conditions: The highway (NH-334 and then Delhi-Meerut Expressway) is generally in good condition, offering a smooth drive.

Route: The typical route is Roorkee - Muzaffarnagar - Meerut - Ghaziabad - Delhi. The Delhi-Meerut Expressway significantly reduces travel time once you reach it.

Distance: The distance from Roorkee to Delhi is approximately 200 km, usually taking 4-5 hours depending on traffic.

Enjoy your journey from Roorkee to Delhi, experiencing the transition from a historic town to the bustling capital city!

Mussoorie to Delhi

Interesting facts to keep in mind when travelling from Mussoorie to Delhi

Food options: Descending from Mussoorie, you'll find good food options in Dehradun, and then along the highway through Roorkee, Muzaffarnagar, and Meerut.

Accommodation options: Dehradun offers many choices if you wish to break the journey early. Further down, Roorkee and Meerut also have hotels.

Toll booths: Expect several toll plazas after Dehradun on the way to Delhi.

Petrol pumps and rest stops: Available in Dehradun and then regularly along the highway.

Road conditions: The road from Mussoorie to Dehradun is a hill road. From Dehradun onwards, highways are generally good, with the Delhi-Meerut Expressway improving the final stretch.

Route: Mussoorie - Dehradun - Roorkee - Muzaffarnagar - Meerut - Ghaziabad - Delhi.

Distance: Approximately 290-310 km, this journey can take 7-9 hours due to the initial hilly terrain and potential traffic.

The journey from the Queen of Hills, Mussoorie, to Delhi transitions from mountain vistas to the plains of North India.

Delhi to Mussoorie

Interesting facts to keep in mind when travelling from Delhi to Mussoorie

Food options: Plenty of food options are available on the plains, especially around Meerut, Muzaffarnagar, and Roorkee. Dehradun also has many good restaurants before the ascent to Mussoorie.

Accommodation options: Consider stopping in Dehradun if you want to break the journey before the hill climb. Mussoorie has a wide variety of hotels with scenic views.

Best time to visit: September to November and March to June are ideal. Winters can be very cold with snowfall.

Road conditions: Highways to Dehradun are good. The road from Dehradun to Mussoorie is a winding hill road and needs careful driving.

Route: Delhi - Ghaziabad - Meerut - Muzaffarnagar - Roorkee - Dehradun - Mussoorie.

Visiting Haridwar

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Haridwar

Food options: Haridwar is famous for its vegetarian cuisine. Try local sweets, poori-sabzi, and lassi. Many restaurants offer North Indian thalis.

Accommodation options: A wide range from budget guesthouses and dharamshalas to mid-range and luxury hotels are available, especially near Har Ki Pauri and the railway station.

Attractions: Key attractions include Har Ki Pauri (for Ganga Aarti), Mansa Devi Temple (via cable car), Chandi Devi Temple, Maya Devi Temple, and various ashrams.

Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, cycle-rickshaws, and local buses are common. Walking is often the best way to explore crowded areas near the ghats.

Best time to visit: October to March offers pleasant weather. Major festivals like Kumbh Mela (every 12 years) and Ardh Kumbh Mela (every 6 years) attract huge crowds.

Respectful Conduct: Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and ghats. Photography might be restricted in some temple interiors.

Haridwar is one of the seven holiest places for Hindus, offering a deeply spiritual and cultural experience.

Visiting Rishikesh

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Rishikesh

Food options: Rishikesh offers a variety of vegetarian food, including many cafes catering to international tourists with continental and healthy options. Local Indian food is also widely available.

Accommodation options: From ashrams offering basic stays to guesthouses, hotels, yoga retreats, and riverside camps, Rishikesh has diverse lodging choices.

Attractions: Famous for Laxman Jhula, Ram Jhula, The Beatles Ashram, Triveni Ghat (Ganga Aarti), Neelkanth Mahadev Temple. It's also a hub for yoga, meditation, and adventure sports like white-water rafting and bungee jumping.

Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, shared Vikram tempos, and scooters for rent are common. Walking is great for exploring areas around the jhulas.

Best time to visit: September to April is generally pleasant. Rafting season is typically from September/October to May/June.

Spiritual Etiquette: Rishikesh is a holy town; alcohol and non-vegetarian food are generally not permitted in many areas. Dress modestly, especially when visiting ashrams and temples.

Rishikesh, the "Yoga Capital of the World," offers a unique blend of spirituality, adventure, and natural beauty.

Visiting Dehradun

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Dehradun

Food options: Dehradun has a diverse culinary scene, from local Garhwali cuisine to popular North Indian, Tibetan (momos are famous), and continental dishes. Bakeries are also quite popular.

Accommodation options: A wide range of hotels, guesthouses, and resorts are available to suit various budgets.

Attractions: Key sights include Robber's Cave (Guchhupani), Sahastradhara (waterfalls and sulphur springs), Forest Research Institute (FRI), Tapkeshwar Temple, Mindrolling Monastery, and the Tibetan Market.

Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws, local buses ("Vikrams"), and taxis are readily available for getting around the city.

Best time to visit: March to June and September to November offer pleasant weather. Winters are cool, and monsoons (July-August) bring heavy rainfall.

Shopping: Paltan Bazaar is famous for local handicrafts, woolens, and souvenirs. Rajpur Road has more modern shopping outlets.

Dehradun, nestled in the Doon Valley, is known for its scenic beauty, pleasant climate, and prestigious educational institutions.

Visiting Mussoorie

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Mussoorie

Food options: Mussoorie offers a range of dining options, from local eateries serving Tibetan and North Indian food to cafes and restaurants on Mall Road with multi-cuisine menus.

Accommodation options: A wide variety of hotels, guesthouses, and resorts are available, many offering stunning valley or mountain views. Book in advance during peak season.

Attractions: Popular spots include Kempty Falls, Gun Hill (via cable car for panoramic views), Mall Road, Lal Tibba, Company Garden, Cloud's End, and Landour.

Local Transport: Taxis, cycle-rickshaws (on Mall Road), and walking are the primary ways to get around. Private vehicles may have restricted access to Mall Road at certain times.

Best time to visit: March to June and September to November. Winters (December-February) are cold and can experience snowfall, offering a different charm.

Things to Do: Enjoy leisurely walks, boating at Mussoorie Lake, shopping for souvenirs, and soaking in the beautiful Himalayan views.

Mussoorie, the "Queen of Hills," is a popular hill station known for its colonial charm, scenic beauty, and pleasant weather.

Visiting Nainital

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Nainital

Food options: Nainital has many restaurants and cafes around Mall Road and near the lake, serving North Indian, Kumaoni, Tibetan, and continental cuisines. Don't miss trying local sweets and snacks.

Accommodation options: A wide range of hotels, guesthouses, and resorts are available, from budget to luxury, many offering lake views. Booking early is advised during peak season.

Attractions: Key attractions include Naini Lake (boating), Naina Devi Temple, Mall Road, Snow View Point (via cable car), Tiffin Top, Nainital Zoo, and nearby lakes like Bhimtal and Sattal.

Local Transport: Cycle-rickshaws, taxis, and walking are common. Private vehicles might have restricted entry to Mall Road during peak hours.

Best time to visit: March to June and September to November. Winters can be cold with occasional snowfall.

Shopping: Mall Road is famous for candles, shawls, wooden handicrafts, and local jams/jellies.

Nainital, a charming hill station built around the beautiful Naini Lake, is a perfect getaway for nature lovers and families.

Visiting Auli

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Auli

Food options: Options are somewhat limited in Auli itself, mainly available at GMVN guesthouses and private resorts. Joshimath, the base town, has more eateries.

Accommodation options: GMVN (Garhwal Mandal Vikas Nigam) has guesthouses in Auli. Some private resorts are also available. Many visitors prefer to stay in Joshimath and make a day trip to Auli via cable car or road.

Attractions: Auli is renowned for its ski slopes (best from January to March), the Auli Ropeway (one of Asia's longest), Gorson Bugyal (meadow), Auli Artificial Lake, and panoramic views of Himalayan peaks like Nanda Devi and Trishul.

Local Transport: Getting around Auli itself is mostly by foot. The ropeway from Joshimath is a key mode of access. Taxis can be hired in Joshimath.

Best time to visit: For skiing, January to March. For pleasant weather and trekking, May to June and September to November.

Altitude: Auli is at a high altitude (around 2,500 to 3,050 meters). Acclimatize properly and be aware of altitude sickness symptoms.

Auli is a premier skiing destination in India, offering breathtaking views and adventure opportunities in the Garhwal Himalayas.

Visiting Jim Corbett National Park

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Jim Corbett National Park

Food options: Most resorts and hotels in and around Ramnagar (the main town near Corbett) have in-house restaurants. Some local eateries are also available in Ramnagar.

Accommodation options: A wide range of options from forest rest houses (inside the park, book well in advance), private resorts, hotels, and guesthouses are available in areas like Dhikuli, Garjia, and Ramnagar.

Attractions: The main attraction is wildlife safaris (Jeep and Canter) in different zones of the park (Bijrani, Jhirna, Dhela, Dhikala, Durga Devi, Sitabani) to spot tigers, elephants, deer, birds, and other wildlife. Corbett Museum and Garjia Temple are also popular.

Safari Bookings: Safaris must be booked in advance online through the official Uttarakhand Tourism website. Permits are limited.

Best time to visit: November to June. The park is partially or fully closed during the monsoon season (typically July to mid-October).

Park Rules: Follow all park rules strictly, maintain silence, do not litter, and do not get down from the safari vehicle unless permitted by the guide.

Jim Corbett National Park, India's oldest national park, is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers, famous for its Bengal tiger population.

Chardham Yatra Overview

Interesting facts for Chardham Yatra (Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, Badrinath)

Food options: Basic vegetarian food (dal, roti, sabzi, rice) is available at dhabas and guesthouses along the Yatra route. Carry some dry snacks.

Accommodation options: Basic guesthouses, dharamshalas, and some budget hotels are available at key locations. GMVN guesthouses are a reliable option. Book in advance, especially during peak season.

Health & Fitness: The Yatra involves trekking at high altitudes. Good physical fitness is essential. Consult a doctor before undertaking the Yatra, especially if you have pre-existing health conditions.

Acclimatization: Proper acclimatization is crucial to avoid altitude sickness. Plan your itinerary with adequate rest days.

Best time to visit: May to June and September to October/November. The shrines are closed during winter due to heavy snowfall (typically from Diwali till Akshaya Tritiya).

Registration: Biometric registration is mandatory for all pilgrims. This can be done online or at various registration counters.

Clothing: Carry warm woolens, rain gear, and comfortable trekking shoes.

The Chardham Yatra is a significant Hindu pilgrimage to four holy shrines nestled in the Himalayas, offering spiritual solace and breathtaking natural beauty.

Visiting Badrinath

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Badrinath

Food options: Vegetarian food is readily available in Badrinath town. Many small restaurants and dhabas serve North Indian meals. Prasad is offered at the temple.

Accommodation options: A variety of guesthouses, hotels, and dharamshalas are available. GMVN has tourist rest houses. Booking in advance is highly recommended during Yatra season.

Attractions: The main Badrinath Temple (dedicated to Lord Vishnu), Tapt Kund (hot thermal springs), Brahma Kapal, Charanpaduka, Mana Village (last Indian village near China border), Vasudhara Falls, and Bheem Pul.

Temple Darshan: Be prepared for queues, especially during peak hours. The temple usually opens early in the morning.

Best time to visit: May to June and September to October/early November. The temple closes for winter.

Altitude: Badrinath is at an altitude of about 3300 meters (10,827 feet). Acclimatize properly.

Badrinath, one of the four Chardham sites, is a revered pilgrimage destination surrounded by majestic snow-capped peaks.

Visiting Kedarnath

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Kedarnath

Food options: Basic vegetarian food is available at small eateries and camps in Kedarnath and along the trek route from Gaurikund.

Accommodation options: Limited options available in Kedarnath, mainly tents, pre-fabricated huts, and some guesthouses. GMVN provides accommodation. Many pilgrims stay at Sonprayag or Guptkashi.

Attractions: The ancient Kedarnath Temple (dedicated to Lord Shiva), Shankaracharya Samadhi, Bhairavnath Temple. The trek itself offers stunning Himalayan views.

Trek: The main access to Kedarnath is via a challenging 16-18 km trek from Gaurikund. Pony, palki (palanquin), and helicopter services are also available (book helicopter well in advance).

Best time to visit: May to June and September to October. The temple is closed during winter due to heavy snow.

Altitude & Fitness: Kedarnath is at about 3583 meters (11,755 feet). High physical fitness and proper acclimatization are essential for the trek.

Kedarnath, a key Chardham shrine, is a powerful spiritual site located amidst breathtaking and rugged Himalayan scenery.

Visiting Gangotri

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Gangotri

Food options: Vegetarian food is available at dhabas and guesthouses in Gangotri town.

Accommodation options: Guesthouses, ashrams, and some budget hotels are available. GMVN also has facilities. Options are relatively basic.

Attractions: Gangotri Temple (dedicated to Goddess Ganga), Bhagirath Shila, Pandava Gufa. The trek to Gaumukh (source of the Ganges) starts from Gangotri (requires permit).

Road Access: Gangotri is accessible by road, though the journey can be long and through mountainous terrain.

Best time to visit: May to June and September to October/early November. The temple closes for the winter.

Altitude: Gangotri is situated at an altitude of about 3100 meters (10,200 feet). Acclimatize properly.

Gangotri, the origin of the holy River Ganga (as Bhagirathi), is a sacred pilgrimage site revered by Hindus, set amidst stunning Himalayan landscapes.

Visiting Yamunotri

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Yamunotri

Food options: Basic vegetarian meals are available at small eateries in Yamunotri and Janki Chatti (the base for the trek).

Accommodation options: Limited guesthouses and dharamshalas are available at Yamunotri and Janki Chatti. GMVN facilities are also present.

Attractions: Yamunotri Temple (dedicated to Goddess Yamuna), Surya Kund (hot water spring where rice and potatoes are cooked as prasad), Divya Shila.

Trek: Yamunotri requires a trek of about 5-6 km from Janki Chatti. Ponies and palkis are available.

Best time to visit: May to June and September to October/early November. The temple closes during winter.

Altitude: Yamunotri is at an altitude of around 3293 meters (10,804 feet). The trek involves ascent, so fitness is important.

Yamunotri, the source of the River Yamuna, is the westernmost shrine in the Chardham circuit, known for its thermal springs and scenic beauty.

Visiting Hemkund Sahib

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Hemkund Sahib

Food options: Langar (free community kitchen) is served at Gurudwara Hemkund Sahib and Gurudwara Gobind Dham (Ghangaria). Basic eateries are available in Ghangaria.

Accommodation options: Accommodation is available at Gurudwaras in Gobindghat and Gobind Dham (Ghangaria). Some private guesthouses are also in Ghangaria. No accommodation at Hemkund Sahib itself.

Attractions: Gurudwara Hemkund Sahib (a high-altitude Sikh pilgrimage site), the pristine Hemkund Lake, Lokpal Temple (dedicated to Lakshman). The Valley of Flowers trek also starts from Ghangaria.

Trek: Hemkund Sahib involves a steep trek of about 6 km from Ghangaria, which itself is a 13-14 km trek from Gobindghat. Ponies and palkis are available.

Best time to visit: Late May/June to early October. The shrine is closed during winter due to heavy snow.

Altitude & Fitness: Hemkund Sahib is at a very high altitude of about 4632 meters (15,197 feet). Excellent physical fitness and acclimatization are crucial.

Hemkund Sahib is a revered Sikh Gurudwara located beside a glacial lake, attracting pilgrims and trekkers with its spiritual significance and stunning alpine setting.

Visiting Tungnath

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Tungnath

Food options: Basic food (tea, snacks, simple meals) is available at small shops in Chopta (the base for the trek) and a few stalls along the Tungnath trek route. Limited options at Tungnath itself.

Accommodation options: Basic guesthouses, lodges, and campsites are available in Chopta. Very limited, basic lodging might be found near Tungnath temple during peak season.

Attractions: Tungnath Temple (highest Shiva temple in the world, one of the Panch Kedar), Chandrashila Peak (1.5 km further trek from Tungnath offering panoramic Himalayan views).

Trek: A moderate trek of about 3.5-4 km from Chopta leads to Tungnath temple.

Best time to visit: April to June and September to November. The temple is closed during winter due to snow.

Altitude: Tungnath is at an altitude of about 3680 meters (12,073 feet). Chandrashila is even higher.

Tungnath offers a spiritually uplifting experience combined with breathtaking views of the Himalayas, making it popular among pilgrims and trekkers.

Visiting Devprayag

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Devprayag

Food options: Local restaurants and dhabas in Devprayag town offer North Indian vegetarian meals.

Accommodation options: Guesthouses and some budget hotels are available in Devprayag. Options are relatively simple.

Attractions: The Sangam (confluence of Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers, which then forms the Ganga), Raghunathji Temple, Suspension Bridges. The sight of the two distinct rivers merging is a major highlight.

Accessibility: Devprayag is well-connected by road and lies on the main route to Badrinath and Kedarnath (via different diversions).

Best time to visit: Throughout the year, though monsoons (July-August) can bring heavy rains. Winters are cool, summers are warm.

Spiritual Significance: Devprayag is one of the Panch Prayag (five confluences) and holds immense religious importance for Hindus.

Devprayag is a sacred town known for the holy confluence of two major Himalayan rivers, offering a serene and spiritually charged atmosphere.

Visiting Jageshwar Dham

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Jageshwar Dham

Food options: Small local eateries and dhabas are available near the temple complex and in nearby Jageshwar village, serving simple North Indian and Kumaoni food.

Accommodation options: KMVN (Kumaon Mandal Vikas Nigam) has a tourist rest house. Some guesthouses and small lodges are also available in and around Jageshwar.

Attractions: The Jageshwar temple complex, consisting of about 124 large and small ancient stone temples (dating from 7th to 12th century AD), dedicated mainly to Lord Shiva. Dandeshwar Temple and Archaeological Museum are also nearby.

Architecture: The temples are an excellent example of Nagara style architecture with intricate carvings.

Best time to visit: March to June and September to November. The Maha Shivaratri festival is celebrated with great fervor.

Location: Situated in the Kumaon region, amidst dense deodar forests, offering a tranquil and spiritual environment.

Jageshwar Dham is a significant pilgrimage site renowned for its cluster of ancient temples and serene natural surroundings in the Almora district.

Trekking to Gaumukh

Interesting facts to keep in mind for the Gaumukh Trek

Food options: Carry your own food supplies and energy snacks. Basic meals might be available at Bhojbasa (the last stop before Gaumukh with accommodation).

Accommodation options: Limited options at Bhojbasa (GMVN guesthouse and some ashram/tent facilities). Camping might be possible with permits. No accommodation at Gaumukh.

Attractions: The trek itself through stunning Himalayan scenery, the snout of the Gangotri Glacier (Gaumukh, the source of Bhagirathi/Ganga), views of peaks like Shivling and Bhagirathi group.

Trek Details: A challenging trek of about 18-19 km from Gangotri to Gaumukh. Usually done over 2 days with an overnight halt at Bhojbasa (14 km from Gangotri).

Permits: Permits are required from the District Forest Officer, Uttarkashi, or online. Limited permits are issued per day.

Best time to visit: May to June and September to mid-October. The area is prone to landslides during monsoon.

Altitude & Fitness: Gaumukh is at an altitude of about 3890 meters (12,760 feet). High physical fitness, proper gear, and acclimatization are essential.

The Gaumukh trek is a rewarding experience for seasoned trekkers and pilgrims, leading to the very source of the holy River Ganga.
