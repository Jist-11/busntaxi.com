Trekking to Gaumukh

Interesting facts to keep in mind for the Gaumukh Trek

Food options: Carry your own food supplies and energy snacks. Basic meals might be available at Bhojbasa (the last stop before Gaumukh with accommodation).

Accommodation options: Limited options at Bhojbasa (GMVN guesthouse and some ashram/tent facilities). Camping might be possible with permits. No accommodation at Gaumukh.

Attractions: The trek itself through stunning Himalayan scenery, the snout of the Gangotri Glacier (Gaumukh, the source of Bhagirathi/Ganga), views of peaks like Shivling and Bhagirathi group.

Trek Details: A challenging trek of about 18-19 km from Gangotri to Gaumukh. Usually done over 2 days with an overnight halt at Bhojbasa (14 km from Gangotri).

Permits: Permits are required from the District Forest Officer, Uttarkashi, or online. Limited permits are issued per day.

Best time to visit: May to June and September to mid-October. The area is prone to landslides during monsoon.

Altitude & Fitness: Gaumukh is at an altitude of about 3890 meters (12,760 feet). High physical fitness, proper gear, and acclimatization are essential.

The Gaumukh trek is a rewarding experience for seasoned trekkers and pilgrims, leading to the very source of the holy River Ganga.
