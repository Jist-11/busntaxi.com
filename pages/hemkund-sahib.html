Visiting Hemkund Sahib

Key Information for Pilgrims and Visitors

Location: Hemkund Sahib is a revered Sikh pilgrimage site located at an altitude of 4,329 meters in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, surrounded by seven snow-capped peaks and a glacial lake.

Access: The trek to Hemkund Sahib starts from Govindghat, with a 19 km route via Ghangaria. The path is steep and scenic, passing through beautiful valleys and waterfalls.

Best Time to Visit: June to October, when the snow has melted and the Gurudwara is open to pilgrims.

Accommodation: Basic lodges and Gurudwara accommodations are available at Ghangaria. No overnight stay is allowed at Hemkund Sahib itself.

Attractions: The star-shaped Gurudwara, Hemkund Lake, and the nearby Valley of Flowers National Park.

Tips: Prepare for high altitude, carry warm clothing, and start the trek early in the morning. The weather can change rapidly.

Hemkund Sahib is a place of immense spiritual significance and natural beauty, attracting thousands of devotees and trekkers every year.
