Visiting Gangotri

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Gangotri

Food options: Vegetarian food is available at dhabas and guesthouses in Gangotri town.

Accommodation options: Guesthouses, ashrams, and some budget hotels are available. GMVN also has facilities. Options are relatively basic.

Attractions: Gangotri Temple (dedicated to Goddess Ganga), Bhagirath Shila, Pandava Gufa. The trek to Gaumukh (source of the Ganges) starts from Gangotri (requires permit).

Road Access: Gangotri is accessible by road, though the journey can be long and through mountainous terrain.

Best time to visit: May to June and September to October/early November. The temple closes for the winter.

Altitude: Gangotri is situated at an altitude of about 3100 meters (10,200 feet). Acclimatize properly.

Gangotri, the origin of the holy River Ganga (as Bhagirathi), is a sacred pilgrimage site revered by Hindus, set amidst stunning Himalayan landscapes.
