Visiting Badrinath

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Badrinath

Food options: Vegetarian food is readily available in Badrinath town. Many small restaurants and dhabas serve North Indian meals. Prasad is offered at the temple.

Accommodation options: A variety of guesthouses, hotels, and dharamshalas are available. GMVN has tourist rest houses. Booking in advance is highly recommended during Yatra season.

Attractions: The main Badrinath Temple (dedicated to Lord Vishnu), Tapt Kund (hot thermal springs), Brahma Kapal, Charanpaduka, Mana Village (last Indian village near China border), Vasudhara Falls, and Bheem Pul.

Temple Darshan: Be prepared for queues, especially during peak hours. The temple usually opens early in the morning.

Best time to visit: May to June and September to October/early November. The temple closes for winter.

Altitude: Badrinath is at an altitude of about 3300 meters (10,827 feet). Acclimatize properly.

Badrinath, one of the four Chardham sites, is a revered pilgrimage destination surrounded by majestic snow-capped peaks.
