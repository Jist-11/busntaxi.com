Visiting Yamunotri

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Yamunotri

Food options: Basic vegetarian meals are available at small eateries in Yamunotri and Janki Chatti (the base for the trek).

Accommodation options: Limited guesthouses and dharamshalas are available at Yamunotri and Janki Chatti. GMVN facilities are also present.

Attractions: Yamunotri Temple (dedicated to Goddess Yamuna), Surya Kund (hot water spring where rice and potatoes are cooked as prasad), Divya Shila.

Trek: Yamunotri requires a trek of about 5-6 km from Janki Chatti. Ponies and palkis are available.

Best time to visit: May to June and September to October/early November. The temple closes during winter.

Altitude: Yamunotri is at an altitude of around 3293 meters (10,804 feet). The trek involves ascent, so fitness is important.

Yamunotri, the source of the River Yamuna, is the westernmost shrine in the Chardham circuit, known for its thermal springs and scenic beauty.
