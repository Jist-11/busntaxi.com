Visiting Kedarnath

Interesting facts to keep in mind when visiting Kedarnath

Food options: Basic vegetarian food is available at small eateries and camps in Kedarnath and along the trek route from Gaurikund.

Accommodation options: Limited options available in Kedarnath, mainly tents, pre-fabricated huts, and some guesthouses. GMVN provides accommodation. Many pilgrims stay at Sonprayag or Guptkashi.

Attractions: The ancient Kedarnath Temple (dedicated to Lord Shiva), Shankaracharya Samadhi, Bhairavnath Temple. The trek itself offers stunning Himalayan views.

Trek: The main access to Kedarnath is via a challenging 16-18 km trek from Gaurikund. Pony, palki (palanquin), and helicopter services are also available (book helicopter well in advance).

Best time to visit: May to June and September to October. The temple is closed during winter due to heavy snow.

Altitude & Fitness: Kedarnath is at about 3583 meters (11,755 feet). High physical fitness and proper acclimatization are essential for the trek.

Kedarnath, a key Chardham shrine, is a powerful spiritual site located amidst breathtaking and rugged Himalayan scenery.
